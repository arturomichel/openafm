OpenAFM is a project to build a low cost open source atomic force microscope (AFM). The projected originates from Lego2Nano, an ongoing collaboration between University College London and Tsinghua University in Beijing, in which PhD students are brought together to hack low-cost scientific equipment for use in Chinese schools. We hope that in building low cost and open source versions of traditionally expensive and specialist scientific tools we will enable and empower high school students and individual enthusiasts to learn abut scientific topics by participating in and even leading real scientific research.
What is an Atomic Force Microscope An atomic force microscope is part of a large family of techniques called scanning probe microscopy (SPM). The difference between SPMs and traditional microscopes is that SPMs do not use a lens or optics to create an image, instead they use a scanning stage and a probe to build up an image. By using a very precise scanning stage and very sensitive probe SPM?s can see things much much smaller than a traditional microscope. In fact we can see molecules and even atoms. Atomic force micrscopes use a very fine cantilever probe to measure the height of a sample by pusing against it, similar to the way braile is read.
